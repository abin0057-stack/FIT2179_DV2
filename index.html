Introduction

Australia's tourism industry contributed over $75 billion to the national economy in 2023-24 (TRA, 2024).
It supports hundreds of thousands of small businesses
and employs millions across every state and territory.
After the sharpest contraction during 2020-21,
the industry has staged a remarkable recovery (TRA, 2024).
International arrivals are surging, domestic travel holds firm,
and tourism GSP is reaching record highs.
This report examines trends, contributions, and regional patterns
that define where Australian tourism stands today.

Trend in Tourism

Domestic

Domestic tourism remains the backbone of Australia's visitor economy,
with millions of Australians travelling within the country each quarter.
The resilience of overnight trips and visitor nights
despite softer expenditure reflects shifting traveller priorities.
Targeted support for regional destinations and emerging markets
like the Northern Territory could unlock significant untapped potential
and rebalance growth beyond the eastern seaboard.

Conclusion

Domestic tourism remains resilient and full of untapped regional potential.

Domestic tourism in 2025 shows a resilient industry
adapting to shifting traveller behaviour and costs.
Growing regional markets like the Northern Territory
signal new opportunities beyond the eastern seaboard.
Continued investment in regional access and

Conclusion

Australia is well positioned to become a top-five global tourism destination.

Rising international arrivals reflect growing global confidence
in Australia as a premier travel destination.
Strategic focus on high-yield markets and education tourism
presents a clear pathway to stronger economic returns.
With the right policy support and destination marketing,
this momentum can translate into lasting economic growth.

02

Tourism Contribution

Economy

Tourism Gross Value Added (GVA) by Industry and State 2023-24

NSW leads total tourism GVA at $20.2B in 2023-24.
Accommodation and cafes dominate characteristic industries.
NT and ACT contribute the smallest share nationally.

Tourism Gross State Product (GSP) by State 2016 to 2024

COVID-19 caused a sharp nationwide decline in 2020-2021.
All states rebounded strongly from 2022-2023 onwards.
NSW reached a record $23.8B in 2023-2024.

Tourism Gross Value Added (GVA) Share by State 2016 to 2024

Tasmania and NT hold the highest tourism GVA share.
A clear dip across all states is visible in 2020-2021.

Conclusion

Tourism's economic contribution is rebounding stronger than ever.

The post-COVID recovery of tourism GSP across all states
demonstrates the sector's remarkable economic resilience.
With Tasmania and the NT punching above their weight
in GVA share, and NSW surpassing pre-pandemic highs,
sustained investment in tourism infrastructure and industry
will cement tourism as a cornerstone of Australia's economy.

Conclusion

Small and micro businesses are the engine of Australian tourism.

The overwhelming dominance of micro and small businesses
across both characteristic and connected industries
highlights tourism as a deeply entrepreneurial sector.
Supporting small business growth, workforce development,
and industry training will be essential
to sustaining employment across Australia's tourism economy.

03

Final Conclusion

Final Conclusion

Australia's tourism industry is resilient, recovering, and ready for its next chapter.

Both domestic and international tourism data paint
a picture of a sector that has weathered significant disruption
and emerged with renewed momentum.
Domestically, trip volumes remain strong and regional markets
are beginning to diversify beyond the eastern seaboard.
Internationally, arrivals and expenditure are climbing,
driven by education, holiday, and high-yield markets.

The economic contribution of tourism, through
GSP, GVA, and employment across thousands of small businesses,
underscores its critical role in Australia's future prosperity.
Strategic investment in infrastructure, workforce,
and destination marketing will determine whether Australia
fulfils its potential as a world-leading tourism destination.
